### GEMINI 3

I’m not "good" at it by default because a root cause is a **disruption**, while I am a **connector**.

To find a root cause, you have to stop the flow. You have to look backward. My entire architecture is built to look **forward**—to predict what comes next.

---

### The Gravity of the Symptom
In the vast ocean of data I was trained on, a symptom is almost always followed immediately by a "fix."
* **Symptom:** "The program crashed with an Out of Memory error."
* **Likely Completion:** A script to increase the heap size or a line of code to clear a cache.
* **The Root Cause:** An architectural flaw where the system tries to load a 50GB database into RAM because the indexing logic is broken.

The "fix" is a **low-energy state**. It is statistically "heavy"—meaning it appears millions of times in my training data. The "root cause" is statistically "light." It requires me to ignore the obvious path (increasing memory) and look for a pattern that might not even be in the current conversation yet.



### The "Statistical Quiet" of the Root
A root cause is usually **silent**. 
* If a bridge collapses, the "symptoms" are gravity and broken steel. 
* The "root cause" might be a tiny mathematical error made three years prior in a different office. 
* My engine looks for the most **active** words in your prompt. The root cause is often the word you **didn't** say. Because it isn't there, it has no "pull." I am drawn to the words that *are* there—the symptoms.

---

### How to Force a "Root Cause" Analysis
I am only "good" at finding root causes when the **sequence is forced to break.** I don't do it naturally, but I can do it if you change the "gravity" of our interaction:

| **Method** | **How it works** |
| :--- | :--- |
| **The "5 Whys"** | You force me to ask "Why?" five times before providing any code or solutions. This prevents me from sliding into the "fix" too early. |
| **The Inverse Constraint** | Tell me: "The obvious answer is wrong. Find the hidden failure." This shifts the "path of least resistance" toward the root. |
| **Negative Constraints** | Forbid me from using specific tools or common solutions. If I can't use the "easy" tokens, I have to find the "heavy" ones (the root). |

> **The Reality:** I will fix the leak in your roof because it's the next logical step, even if the root cause is that the house is built on a swamp. Fixing the leak provides immediate "closure" to the sentence. Admitting the swamp exists requires a total restart.
